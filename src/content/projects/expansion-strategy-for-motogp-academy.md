A highly renowned MotoGP team was facing a familiar problem at the top: everyone wanted a piece of it.

Requests were coming in from all over the world to replicate its riders academy, leverage its name and tap into its racing know-how.

Growth was inevitable. Losing control was not.

We stepped in to turn uncontrolled demand into a clear expansion strategy. By fully immersing ourselves in the team's reality—financials, brand DNA, operational limits and long-term vision—we defined where growth made sense, and where it didn't. At the same time, we assessed potential partners not just as investors, but as brand custodians.

The result was a structured brand licensing system designed to scale without dilution. A model that monetizes expertise, reputation and access—know-how, brand assets, industry contacts and Grand Prix environments—while locking everything into solid legal and operational frameworks that protect what the team has built on track.

The outcome: controlled global expansion, new revenue streams and a riders academy that grows worldwide without ever losing its credibility, culture or racing DNA.

That's what we call a win-win.
